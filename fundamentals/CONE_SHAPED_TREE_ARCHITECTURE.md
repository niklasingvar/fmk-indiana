# CONE-SHAPED TREE ARCHITECTURE

## Definition
- Every file sits on a stability gradient — purpose, architecture, rules, preferences, learnings — most stable at the top.

## Rules
- Knowledge flows up the gradient over time, compressing at every step — wide base, narrow top.
- When two files disagree, the more stable layer wins, always.

## Test
- The top layers are few and short; a fat top or a conflict resolved downward breaks the cone.

## Incorporation
- This repo: the layer scheme L0–L6 in [docs/context-model/CONTEXT-MODEL.md](../docs/context-model/CONTEXT-MODEL.md) §1–§2.
- System prompt: the context-model seed ships the gradient and its conflict rule to every monitored repo.
